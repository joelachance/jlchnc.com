# Not Advice

Steven Spielberg didn't get into film school. He was rejected by USC 3 times.
As he tells it, he took the Universal Studios tram tour and slipped off at the sound stages.
Someone gave him a day pass, and he came back the next day in a suit and tie.
He then came back the next day. And the next. Every day for the rest of the summer.

He found an unused office, bought a plastic name tag, and listed himself in the building directory.

About a year ago, I tried to find a mentor. I wanted someone to help me navigate decisions as a first time founder.
I took an intro call with one potential mentor, and he told me he has no idea what I should be doing. This was from
someone who founded a company before! Shouldn't he have some idea? He told me I was the only one who knew what I
should do.

He was right. No one can give you advice on your vision. You're the only one who can see it!
So none of this is advice, but the building blocks in creating your vision.

## Creating Your Vision
Creating something requires you to have a vision. Ideally you have some idea of what you want to create.
Maybe it's a job or a product. The more specific the vision, the better. In order to make your vision a reality,
you need act like it already exists.

Acting like your vision exists can be difficult. After all, your idea hasn't been done before, it's a moonshot. Since
you haven't seen it done before, you might do some crazy shit like pretending to work at Universal! You don't know
how to create what you want yet, so you might as well try some half court shots.

After Tony Hawk landed the first 900 in skateboarding, everyone started landing 900s. Once people saw it was possible,
it got a lot easier to land 900s. Once you see someone create something, it becomes a lot easier for you
to do the same. It's easier to believe you can create something if you've already seen it.

## Be Delusional
There's one more thing you need in order to will your vision into existence: you have to be delusional.

All successful people were at one point delusional. Speilberg put his name in the directory when he wasn't even an employee.
Since we all know how successful he was, if you squint it looks a lot like confidence. In realtime, it's delusion.

The more delusional you can be, the better. Because a healthy amount of delusion will get you through the valley.

## The Valley
The valley is the gap when you have to act delusional long enough for reality to catch up to you. Your belief works a lot
faster than reality, so you have to give it some time.

The valley is when most people quit. You're going to have doubts. Just keep going! Keep acting the part. As long as you
don't give up, the valley will eventually end. It ended for Speilberg, when at 22 he was signed to a 7 year contract.

Whatever you envision is exactly what reality is going to give you. Be careful what you wish for, you might just get it.
