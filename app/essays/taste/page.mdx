# On Taste

Taste has always been the most important part of any creative endeavor, whether
that's products, music, or art. Marketing is easier when design and taste are
good. Taste draws users to a product. Taste is what helps people identify with
something. It's what draws you to a specific song. It's the feeling of
resonance when you use something for the first time and you find satisfaction
or emotion in using it.

It's funny to me that this gets talked about more as if it's now the only thing
that matters. It was always the only thing that mattered.

Marketing is trying to communicate taste. Marketing is trying to tell you, "Hey,
look at this product. We have good taste. You would like it too."

Sales is the same. "Hey, we understand your problem and we know how to fix it.
We did it in such a way that you will like it. It will match your taste."

Taste is much more obvious in art and music, where the product is very
subjective. You either like Taylor Swift or you dislike her, and while some
people may seem indifferent, I don't think people are actually as indifferent
as they make themselves out to be.

You know what you like. One of my favorite bands is Led Zeppelin, and I know
exactly what I like about Led Zeppelin.

It's not the music and it's not the lyrics. It's the feeling I get when I
listen to it.

You've had this feeling. It's the same feeling you get when you use a product
that just works well. Maybe that was an Apple product. Maybe it was the first
Nintendo you got for Christmas. Maybe it was a sports car.

You probably weren't able to communicate the intricacies of the design or the
experience that felt so right to you, but you could feel it. I know you know
the feeling.

This is the only feeling I'm striving for, and while things are getting easier
to build with AI, taste is something that can't be replaced. It's something
that can't be farmed out to another person or a machine.

Taste comes from you. It comes from your personality, from your past
experiences, from the vision of who you're becoming.

No one can duplicate your taste, and no one can truly copy it.

Your taste comes from your experiences with life. It might be from other
products or experiences or interactions with others, and those are always
aggregated, recycled, and recreated through your perspective. That is
creativity. In that creativity, in a way, you couldn't untangle lies from your
taste.
